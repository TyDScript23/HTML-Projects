Germany (1721): Bach writes the Brandenburg Concertos

Bach published his most known works to date inspired by all of his most famous musical scores prior to writing the piece. He presents it to Christian Ludwig. Each song was meticulously conducted to have its own playing style and was first played by an ensemble of seven players.

United States (1901): Charles "Buddy" Bolden organizes the first jazz band

Buddy Bolden, a very charismatic and popular cornetist, played the first New Orleans jazz and made his own unique playing style. His music gave a genre that the general populus could dance and was credited as the birthplace of jazz that would be played and performed for over a century.

Nigeria (1969): Fela Kuti created Afrobeat

With his drummer, Tony Allen, Kuti experiemented with various forms of contemporary music and created his own signature style called Afrobeat that became very popular in the 1970s. This genre would later go on to inspire jazz and other modern genres in the 1990s and 2000s.

Japan (589): Gagaku Asain Classical music is Introduced

As the buddhist religion from China was introduced into Japan, so did the musical style of Gagaku, which was traditionally played on instruments such as the koto or biwa. This genre of music became a folk standard in Asia for thousands of years and is still widely recognized today.

India (1931): Bollywood is Introduced in Film

Since the first sound film was introduced, India has developed their own style of music to accompany it. Set in the style of an opera, Bollywood was created specifically for performance during or after films and had become a staple of Indian cinema for five centuries and one of the most well-known forms of music from South-East Asia.
